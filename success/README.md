# Factor(s) for success

## Context

Four year old kids are placed alone in a room. In front of them there is a marshmallow. They had to stay in the room for 15 minutes.

- If the kid did not eat the marshmallow, they'd get another one.

To tell a four year old kid to wait for 15 minutes for something that they like is like telling an adult to wait for coffee for 2 hours.

## Problems

- 2/3 ate the marshmallow.
  - Some left it for 14 1/2 minutes, but finally ate it.
- 1/3 would get up, walk around, play around, etc.

## Solution

The ability to delay gratification / self-discipline.

## Benefits

- 14/15 years later: kids are now 18/19; researchers found out that 100% of those did not eat the marshmallow were successful:
  - good grades;
  - good relationship with their teachers;
  - they were happy;
  - with plans for the future.
- a great percentage of the kids that ate the marshmallow were in trouble: bad grades, no university, dropped out.

## Todo for oneself

Be careful with eagerness. Example: salesperson:

- a client comes and says "I want this"
  - a bad salesperson would give it directly;
  - a good salesperson would first:
    - build-up trust;
    - understand the clients;
    - ensure that what they're asking for is really suiting them;
    - thus, they'll have greater chance of upselling afterwards.
